PAELLA



Paella is a famous Spanish rice dish that originated in Valencia. It is traditionally cooked in a wide, shallow pan and combines saffron-infused rice with ingredients such as seafood, chicken, vegetables, and spices. Paella is known for its vibrant color and rich, savory flavor.

Ingredients:

  • 2 cups short-grain rice
  • 4 cups chicken or seafood broth
  • 1 lb (450 g) chicken thighs, cut into pieces
  • 1/2 lb (225 g) shrimp, peeled and deveined
  • 1 cup mussels or clams
  • 1 red bell pepper, sliced
  • 1 onion, diced
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 tsp paprika
  • 1/4 tsp saffron threads
  • 2 tbsp olive oil
  • Salt and black pepper to taste

Steps to follow:

  1. Heat the olive oil in a large paella pan or skillet.
  2. Cook the chicken until browned on all sides.
  3. Add the onion, bell pepper, and garlic. Cook until softened.
  4. Stir in the paprika and saffron.
  5. Add the rice and stir to coat it in the seasonings.
  6. Pour in the broth and bring to a gentle simmer.
  7. Cook without stirring for about 15 minutes.
  8. Add the shrimp and mussels, then continue cooking until the rice is tender and the seafood is cooked through.
  9. Remove from the heat and let rest for 5 minutes.
  10. Serve directly from the pan.
