TACOS AL PASTOR



Tacos al Pastor are a popular Mexican street food made with thinly sliced pork marinated in a blend of spices, chilies, and pineapple. The meat is traditionally cooked on a vertical spit, then served in warm corn tortillas with onions, cilantro, and fresh pineapple.

Ingredients:

  • 2 lbs (900 g) pork shoulder, thinly sliced
  • 3 dried guajillo chilies, seeded
  • 2 cloves garlic
  • 1/2 cup pineapple juice
  • 2 tbsp white vinegar
  • 1 tsp paprika
  • 1 tsp ground cumin
  • 1 tsp oregano
  • 1 tsp salt
  • 1/2 pineapple, sliced
  • 12 corn tortillas
  • 1/2 cup diced onion
  • 1/2 cup chopped cilantro
  • Lime wedges for serving

Steps to follow:

  1. Soak the dried chilies in hot water for 10 minutes.
  2. Blend the chilies, garlic, pineapple juice, vinegar, paprika, cumin, oregano, and salt into a smooth marinade.
  3. Coat the pork with the marinade and refrigerate for at least 4 hours or overnight.
  4. Cook the pork in a hot skillet or grill until browned and cooked through.
  5. Grill the pineapple slices until lightly caramelized.
  6. Chop the cooked pork and pineapple into small pieces.
  7. Warm the corn tortillas.
  8. Fill each tortilla with pork and pineapple.
  9. Top with diced onion and chopped cilantro.
  10. Serve with lime wedges.
