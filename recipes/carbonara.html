CARBONARA



Carbonara is a traditional Italian pasta dish made with eggs, cheese, pancetta or guanciale, and black pepper. The sauce is created by combining hot pasta with the egg and cheese mixture, resulting in a creamy texture without the use of cream.

Ingredients:

  • 12 oz (340 g) spaghetti
  • 4 oz (115 g) pancetta or guanciale, diced
  • 2 large eggs
  • 1 egg yolk
  • 1 cup grated Parmesan or Pecorino Romano cheese
  • 1 tsp freshly ground black pepper
  • Salt, to taste

Steps to follow:

  1. Bring a large pot of salted water to a boil and cook the spaghetti until al dente.
  2. While the pasta cooks, cook the pancetta in a skillet over medium heat until crispy.
  3. In a bowl, whisk together the eggs, egg yolk, cheese, and black pepper.
  4. Reserve about 1 cup of pasta water, then drain the spaghetti.
  5. Add the hot pasta to the skillet with the pancetta and remove from the heat.
  6. Quickly stir in the egg and cheese mixture, tossing continuously.
  7. Add a little reserved pasta water if needed to create a smooth, creamy sauce.
  8. Serve immediately with extra cheese and black pepper.
